MIT Beaver Works Summer Institute Microelectronics and Hardware Development Course

"The MIT Beaver Works Summer Institute (BWSI) is a rigorous, world-class STEM program for talented students who will be entering their senior year in high school. The four-week program teaches STEM skills through project-based, workshop-style courses." - About BWSI.

I was a Teaching Assistant for the 2024 Microelectronics and Hardware Development Course led by Dr. Beijia Zhang. Right after graduating with a Master's degree, I took this opportunity to teach 40 most extraordinary highschool students from all over the US who were selected by their schools to participate in this course. This helped me polish my skills on various topics and challenged me to solve some interesting technical problems while improving my communication skills to impart technical knowledge.

For this course I developed and delivered coursework and challenges for both theory and lab sessions. Topics for the course included Semiconductor theory, Microcontroller programming, Motor control, Sensor data acquisition and processing, and Semiconductor chip fabrication basics. Following the coursework, the students engaged in a two week long self defined project where I helped students solve problems they faced during their project with hardware and software for their microcontrollers. The projects ranged from Home automation solutions to Medical devices that could help detect seizures.

As a part of this coures I also visited the MIT Nano Semiconductor Fabrication Lab, albeit Virtually.

Watch these projects being presented by the students on the BWSI Project Presentations Website.
