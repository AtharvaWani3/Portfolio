Home-use Neurological Disability Assistive Technology Device

I completed this project under the guidance of Dr. Troy McDaniel in collaboration with Barrow Neurological Institute, Phoenix, AZ.

I led a multidisciplinary team of six students to develop an innovative assistive technology device aimed at aiding patients with chronic hand muscle weakness resulting from stroke or spinal cord injury. Collaborating with Barrow Neurological Institute in Phoenix, AZ, we leveraged their neurology and rehabilitation expertise to enhance our project. Utilizing SolidWorks for CAD designs, I played a crucial role in creating various prototype components. I spearheaded the development of a novel electric stimulation pad, employing rapid prototyping and iterative design to address existing technological issues. By implementing 3D printing techniques, we initially tested fitment and comfort with PLA models and finalized the prototype using SLS 3D printing with metal. This approach eliminated the need for frequent replacement, offering a cost-effective, comfortable, and ergonomic solution. Integrating the Arduino platform ensured seamless connectivity and user-friendly operation. My effective project management led to the project winning the Best Project Award and securing additional funding from Barrow Neurological Institute. Additionally, I initiated a US Patent Application for our technology, emphasizing our commitment to innovation and intellectual property development. Our focus on accessibility resulted in a lightweight, portable, and cost-effective device, and we continue to refine and advance this assistive technology as we await the patent application verdict.
