Prosthetic Hand

This project was made for the AME 598: Expressive Robotics class under the guidance of Dr. Byron Lahey.

My team and I created a prosthetic hand that would mimic the movements of a human hand in real-time.

We modeled and manufactured a Mimicking Hand, seamlessly merging robotics with artistic principles to design innovative prosthetics. Leveraging my expertise in Blender 3.4 and SOLIDWORKS, I crafted a creation that showcases the perfect blend of form and function. Utilizing advanced fabrication techniques, including 3D PLA printing with MakerBot Replicator+ and Creality Ender 3 Pro, along with precise laser cutting using Epilog Helix and Fusion, I ensured high-quality results. Leading a dynamic team of three, we developed an advanced actuator system architecture, effectively replicating human-like movements with servo motors (Arduino). Through a meticulous approach of iterative testing, we overcame design and manufacturing challenges, paving the way for substantial improvements in performance and functionality.
